Sherubtse College Hosts Insightful Talks on AI, Financial Literacy, and Socio-Political Theories

On September 30, 2024, Sherubtse College hosted a comprehensive research workshop and a series of engaging guest lectures featuring distinguished speakers from both local and international academic circles. The event, attended by both students and faculty, provided a platform for insightful discussions on diverse topics ranging from artificial intelligence to literature.

Professor Ngawang Dorji from the Department of English, acted as a key organizer, working in collaboration with the Research Activities faculty members and students from the Population and Development Studies program.

Dr. Bavinder Mehta from the Department of Commerce delivered an insightful presentation on the latest advancements in Artificial Intelligence and Financial Literacy. He highlighted the increasing role of AI in reshaping the financial landscape and its potential to enhance personal financial management and investment strategies.

Professor Karma Choden from the Department of Sociology presented a compelling lecture on the influence of socio-political theories on modern economic systems. Her research delved into the intricate relationship between political ideologies, economic development, wealth distribution, and the welfare of marginalized communities.

Mr. Tandin Wangchuk from the Department of Political Science discussed the significance of political literacy and the role of higher education in fostering an informed and engaged citizenry. He emphasized the importance of critical thinking and active participation in democratic processes.

Dr. Sonam Phuntsho from the Department of English provided a thought-provoking discourse on the intersection of literature and socio-political themes, exploring how literary works reflect and influence societal changes.

The event was a success, fostering active participation from both students and faculty members. The discussions and lectures provided diverse academic fields that intersect in today's complex global environment, enriching the understanding of attendees.
